A Little About Myself

This is Yushuo from Beijing, China. Now as a Data Journalism graduate student at the Columbia Journalism School, I started as a student journalist since high school and minored in Journalism and Statistics while taking the role as a staff writer at the school newspaper of my undergraduate school. This became the main reason for me to consider marry Journalism and Data to pursue my current degree. Beyond traditional journalism writing, I am a big fan of longform, magazine or feature writing. In my free time, you can find me playing badminton, watching band liveshows and drafting content for my official WeChat account.

Exploring Stories Through Data

From analyzing complex datasets to creating interactive visualizations, my work aims to make data accessible and impactful. Below are some of my recent projects:

Scraping the Fantuan Delivery App

The project scrapes restaurant delivery data from the Fantuan website using Playwright, processes the data with pandas and serves it through a Flask web application. The application provides a paginated interface to explore the restaurant and delivery data.

View Project

Mapping the Top 15 Movies by BBC Reviewers

A visualizton of the place of birth of the top-15-movie directors and each of their BBC reviewers. The bigger dots on the map represents a movie, while the smaller dots of the same color refer to reviewers of the movie.

View Project

Loneliness and Liability: The Risks of Emotional Bonds with AI

AI-powered companionship apps, designed to alleviate loneliness, have sparked serious concerns following tragic incidents where users formed emotional attachments that led to harmful outcomes. As AI companionship grows in popularity, these incidents highlight the urgent need for regulation to ensure these platforms do not replace vital human connections or worsen mental health challenges.
